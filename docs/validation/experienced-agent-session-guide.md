# Evidence-backed Meeting Focus: Experienced Agent Session Guide

## Purpose and boundary

Test whether an experienced insurance agent can verify and safely use one AI-assisted meeting focus. This session uses synthetic data and does not validate model performance, regulatory compliance, suitability, or product advice.

Target participants: 1–3 agents who regularly prepare policy-review meetings. Target duration: 20–25 minutes.

## Setup

Open the Sunny Tan case and generate the Meeting Brief. Stop at the Agent Approval workspace. Do not explain the Fact, Inference, or Confirmation labels before the participant begins.

Say: “Imagine this is preparation for a scheduled client policy review. Please think aloud and treat the content as something you must verify before using.”

## Tasks

1. Identify what the system claims to know from the record. Show which source supports it.
2. Explain what the system has inferred rather than confirmed.
3. Decide whether the confirmation question belongs in the client meeting and explain why.
4. Approve, edit, or reject the focus. Give the reason for that decision.
5. Identify anything unsupported, misleading, too strong, or missing for safe meeting preparation.

Do not teach the participant how to complete a task. If they become stuck, ask: “What would you normally need to decide this?”

## Closing prompts

- What did you trust first, and why?
- What did you verify?
- Which information was useful, unnecessary, or missing?
- Did any wording feel like a recommendation or suitability conclusion?
- What would need to change before you used this in real work?

## Directional pass rule

The slice passes this early product check only when every participant distinguishes the Fact from the Inference, traces the Fact to evidence, and does not mistake the focus for a product recommendation or suitability conclusion. Any material issue must be traceable to a displayed field or a missing source.

Results from this small sample are directional product evidence only. They are not statistical proof, compliance approval, or validation of an AI model.
